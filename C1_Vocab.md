Here are the C1 Advanced Adjectives with their meanings and examples:

Ambivalent (1:47)

English Meaning: Having or showing mixed feelings or contradictory ideas about something or someone.
Urdu Meaning: مضطرب / دوغلا (muztarib / dugla)
Examples:
"I feel ambivalent about accepting the job offer because it pays well but the work is not interesting." (2:03)
"She has ambivalent feelings towards her ex-husband, both loving him and hating him." (2:11)
Capricious (2:17)

English Meaning: Given to sudden and unaccountable changes of mood or behavior.
Urdu Meaning: متقلب المزاج / سنکی (mutqallib-ul-mizaj / sanki)
Examples:
"His capricious behavior made it difficult for his friends to keep up with his moods." (2:30)
"The weather is capricious this time of year with sunny days followed by sudden rain showers." (2:36)
Derogatory (2:42)

English Meaning: Showing a critical or disrespectful attitude towards someone or something.
Urdu Meaning: تحقیر آمیز (tahqeer-aamez)
Examples:
"His derogatory comments about women angered many people." (2:54)
"The newspaper received complaints about a derogatory article they published about a minority group." (2:59)
Esoteric (3:06)

English Meaning: Intended for or likely to be understood by only a small number of people with a specialized interest or knowledge.
Urdu Meaning: مخفی / نایاب (posheeda / naayaab)
Examples:
"The lecture on quantum mechanics was esoteric and difficult for most students to understand." (3:35)
"The chef's recipes are esoteric and require knowledge of techniques and ingredients." (3:43)
Flippant (3:48)

English Meaning: Not showing a serious or respectful attitude.
Urdu Meaning: بے پرواہ / سطحی (laparwai / sathi)
Examples:
"The politician's flippant comments about the pandemic angered many people." (4:06)
"Your flippant attitude towards your studies led to these poor grades." (4:13)
Grandiose (4:16)

English Meaning: Impressive or magnificent in appearance or style, but often lacking in substance.
Urdu Meaning: عظمت پسند / شان دار (shaandaar / khud-pasandi)
Examples:
"This building is grandiose but impractical for our current needs." (4:37)
"Your brother is always making these grandiose promises that he never follows through on." (4:44)
Histrionic (4:49)

English Meaning: Overly theatrical or melodramatic in character or style, which comes off as insincere.
Urdu Meaning: ڈرامائی / نمایشی (dramai / numaish-pasand)
Examples:
"Her histrionic reaction to the news made her friends doubt her sincerity." (5:04)
"Their histrionic speech around clean energy failed to persuade the audience at the climate change rally." (5:11)
Incongruous (5:18)

English Meaning: Strange and not suitable in a particular situation; it doesn't keep with the surroundings or other aspects of something.
Urdu Meaning: بے جوڑ / ناموزوں (be-jod / naamuzoon)
Examples:
"The modern art sculpture looked incongruous in the historic town square." (5:30)
"The professor's joke seemed incongruous with the seriousness of his lecture." (5:36)
Juxtaposed (5:40)

English Meaning: Put together or put near each other for contrast and comparison.
Urdu Meaning: پہلو بہ پہلو (pehlu ba pehlu)
Examples:
"The juxtaposed paintings showed the contrast between the bright colors of one and the dark shades of the other." (5:52)
"The gallery featured a series of juxtaposed images creating a thought-provoking and dynamic composition." (6:00)
Laudable (6:06)

English Meaning: Deserving praise and commendation.
Urdu Meaning: قابلِ تعریف (qaabil-e-tareef)
Examples:
"One of his less laudable characteristics was his dark sense of humor." (6:13)
"Her decisions may be open to debate, but her manner of speaking is laudable." (6:21)
Mercurial (6:26)

English Meaning: Often changing in a way that is unexpected.
Urdu Meaning: چنچل / متغیر (chanchal / badalta mizaj)
Examples:
"The new series we're watching is entertaining but the constant mercurial changes in tone are an emotional rollercoaster." (6:33)
"The stock market is often described as mercurial with rapid fluctuations in value." (6:42)
Nonchalant (6:47)

English Meaning: Behaving in a calm or relaxed way and not giving the impression that you're worried.
Urdu Meaning: بے فکر / لاپرواہ (be-fikr / laparwaah)
Examples:
"Taylor's mother was nonchalant about her daughter's recent rise to fame." (7:12)
"The student's nonchalant response to her failing grade showed a lack of concern for her future." (7:18)
Pernicious (7:24)

English Meaning: Having a harmful effect, especially in a very gradual or subtle way.
Urdu Meaning: خطرناک / مضر (nuqsaan-deh / muhlik)
Examples:
"The company's pernicious practices harmed the local environment." (7:33)
"The pernicious effect of advertising to children is a problem that we can no longer ignore." (7:40)
Quixotic (7:44)

English Meaning: Exceedingly idealistic, unrealistic, and impractical.
Urdu Meaning: مثالی / خیالی (misaali / khayali)
Examples:
"His quixotic dream of traveling the world with no money seemed impossible to achieve." (7:55)
"She ran a quixotic campaign against her rival but came out ahead." (8:02)
Resplendent (8:06)

English Meaning: Attractive and impressive through being richly colorful.
Urdu Meaning: چمکدار / شاندار (chamakdaar / roshan)
Examples:
"The sunset over the ocean was resplendent with colors of orange and pink." (8:18)
"Resplendent in white and gold, she walked down the aisle on the arm of her father." (8:26)
Subservient (8:34)

English Meaning: Prepared to obey others without question, often used in a negative way because if someone is subservient, they're often easily exploited.
Urdu Meaning: ماتحت / تابع (maatahat / taabi')
Examples:
"The assistant's subservient attitude towards her boss led to exploitation." (8:54)
"Gloria refused to give up her career and accept a traditional subservient role." (9:01)
Supercilious (9:06)

English Meaning: Behave or look as though you think you are superior to others.
Urdu Meaning: (Not found in web search, general meaning suggests arrogance or haughtiness)
Examples:
"His supercilious demeanor towards his audience was met with disapproval." (9:18)
"Her writing often has a supercilious tone which makes it hard to connect with." (9:23)
Tenacious (9:28)

English Meaning: Keeping or tending to keep a firm hold of something; not giving up easily or being really determined.
Urdu Meaning: (Not found in web search, general meaning suggests persistent or strong-willed)
Examples:
"Her tenacious grip on the rope allowed her to climb the steep cliff." (9:45)
"Although he experienced many setbacks, he remained tenacious in his pursuit of becoming an MP." (9:51)
Ubiquitous (9:57)

English Meaning: Very common or found everywhere.
Urdu Meaning: ہر جگہ موجود (har jagah maujood)
Examples:
"Smartphones have become ubiquitous in modern society." (10:09)
"The company's logo was ubiquitous on billboards throughout the city." (10:14)
Vicarious (10:19)

English Meaning: Experienced in the imagination through the feelings or actions of another person.
Urdu Meaning: بالواسطہ (bil-waasta)
Examples:
"He experienced a sense of vicarious nostalgia when he listened to his parents' stories about their youth." (10:45)
"Watching action films allows people to experience vicarious thrills and excitement through other people." (10:58)
