Here's a list of idioms and phrases discussed in the video, along with their meanings and examples:

Rhyming Reduplication Examples:

Holy moly (3:08): An interjection showing surprise.
Example: "Holy moly, there are a lot of examples."
Nitty-gritty (3:17): The important detail.
Example: "I'm going to show you the nitty-gritty."
Itsy-bitsy (3:22): Very, very small.
Example: "We're going to go from the itsy-bitsy..."
Higgledy-piggledy (3:26): Confused or jumbled.
Example: "...to the higgledy-piggledy."
Bee's knees (3:33): An idiom meaning excellent.
Example: "Trust me, these expressions are the bee's knees."
Okie-dokie (3:41): Okay.
Example: "Okie dokie, let's go."
Hoity-toity (4:22): Behaving like you are better than everyone else.
Example: "I hate all this hoity-toity."
Boogie-woogie (4:33): Dance.
Example: "Let's go out next weekend and boogie woogie."
Fuddy-duddy (4:45): Describes someone or something old-fashioned.
Example: "Don't be such a fuddy-duddy, come out tonight," or "His outfit was a bit fuddy-duddy."
Silly Billy (5:06): What we say to children (or pets) who aren't behaving in a sensible way.
Example: "Oh, you are a silly Billy, take that bucket off your head."
Hocus Pocus (5:41): Silliness or magical activity, often used to trick someone.
Example: "Just be honest, stop all this Hocus Pocus."
Lovey-dovey (6:02): A romantic adjective for people who show PDAs (public displays of affection).
Example: "Mum and dad are far too lovey-dovey; they're always holding hands."
Helter-skelter (6:19): Can be a type of slide or an adjective to describe hurrying and confusion.
Example: "We had a Helter Skelter journey to the show."
Namby-pamby (6:35): Meaning weak, feeble, or excessively emotional. It's a bit cruel.
Example: "What a Namby pamby idea!"
Willy-nilly (6:52): Doing something in an irregular way, without planning or organization.
Example: "The new agency just sends its proposals willy-nilly," or "Don't just do it willy-nilly, think about it first."
Jeepers Creepers (7:16): An exclamation showing surprise or shock, often used to avoid saying something blasphemous.
Example: "Jeepers Creepers, knock on the door before you just come in next time!"

Schm-Reduplication (Yiddish Origin):

Rhyming schmyming (8:54): Used as an intensifier, making the concept more memorable or dismissive.
Money shmoney (9:16): Means money is not important or money's no object.
Homework shmomwork (9:22): Implies there are better things to do than homework.
Bored shmored (9:30): Confrontational, like saying "I don't believe you, you aren't bored."
Fancy schmancy (10:07): Added emphasis, like saying "fancy" in a more elaborate way.
